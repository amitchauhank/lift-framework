# The LIFT Framework
### An AI Adoption Framework for Service-Based Organizations

**Leverage · Integrate · Fortify · Transform**

> *Stop chasing tools. Start solving problems.*

---

## What Is LIFT?

LIFT is a practical, open-source AI adoption framework designed specifically for **service-based organizations** — agencies, consultancies, professional services firms, and any company where the product is people, relationships, and expertise rather than software.

Most AI strategy advice is written for product and technology companies. LIFT is for everyone else.

It is built around a single belief: the right question is not *"what AI tools should we adopt?"* — it is *"what problems are worth solving, and is AI actually the right answer?"*

---

## The Four Stages

| Stage | Name | What It Means |
|-------|------|---------------|
| **L** | **Leverage** | Use your existing strengths to find the right problems. Don't chase new capabilities — mine what you already have. |
| **I** | **Integrate** | Embed AI into actual workflows with real adoption. Make deliberate build vs. buy decisions. |
| **F** | **Fortify** | Build governance, guardrails, and resilience so AI works sustainably — not fragile. |
| **T** | **Transform** | Scale what works into competitive advantage and shift how your organization thinks. |

### The White Paper
The core framework document. Covers the full LIFT model including the maturity model, Problem-Value-Fit canvas, build vs. buy decision framework, pilot design principles, governance structure, and culture change guidance. Also includes a plain-language section on what you can safely ignore in the current AI hype cycle — agents, multi-agent orchestration, and the pressure to build your own AI.

### The Action Toolkit (Excel)
The operational layer of the framework. Eight worksheets that take you from assessment to action:

1. **START HERE** — Navigation and status tracker
2. **Maturity Assessment** — Honest self-assessment across 5 dimensions
3. **Workflow Inventory** — Map and quantify your recurring manual work
4. **Leverage Scoring** — Score workflows for AI fit; identify pilot candidates
5. **Pilot Design Canvas** — Design structured pilots with hypotheses and exit criteria
6. **AI Adoption Gate** — Gate every initiative before it goes live
7. **Governance Checklist** — 20-item foundation across 5 governance categories
8. **Transform Readiness** — Assess readiness to scale AI into competitive advantage

### The Vendor Assessment Guide
A standalone companion for evaluating AI vendors. Three parts:
- **Decision Tree** — Confirm whether you need a vendor at all (Use / Configure / Build)
- **Red Flag Checklist** — 17 signs a vendor may be overselling
- **Scoring Matrix** — Weighted criteria to compare vendors objectively

## How To Use LIFT

**If you're just getting started:**
1. Read the white paper — specifically the "Before We Start" section and the Maturity Model
2. Open the Action Toolkit and complete Tab 1 (Maturity Assessment)
3. Move to Tab 2 and inventory your workflows before touching any AI tool

**If you're evaluating vendors or tools:**
1. Complete the Decision Tree in the Vendor Assessment Guide first
2. Use the Red Flag Checklist in every demo
3. Score finalists using the Scoring Matrix before committing

**If you're building an internal AI strategy:**
1. Use the full toolkit in sequence — each tab feeds the next
2. Share the white paper with leadership to align on language and expectations
3. Use the Governance Checklist to build your policy foundation before scaling

## The Core Principles

- **Problems before tools.** Never start with a tool. Start with a problem worth solving.
- **Automate before you orchestrate.** Most service companies need reliable workflows, not autonomous agents.
- **Prove before you scale.** A pilot without a hypothesis is a hobby.
- **Use before you build.** Competitive advantage comes from how you use AI, not whether you built it.
- **Honesty over optimism.** The maturity model is deliberately blunt. Knowing where you actually are is more valuable than where you wish you were.

---

## A Note on AI Agents and Hype

If your leadership team is asking about AI agents, multi-agent orchestration, or building proprietary AI systems — the white paper addresses this directly in the "Before We Start" section.

The short version: **you are not behind.** Most service companies do not need agents. They need better-designed workflows. Agents provide autonomy. Automation provides reliability. Start with reliability.

## Contributing

LIFT is open source and built to be improved by the people using it.

**Ways to contribute:**
- Open an **Issue** to flag something that's unclear, missing, or wrong
- Submit a **Pull Request** with improved language, additional examples, or new templates
- Share how you've adapted LIFT for your organization — case studies and real-world applications help everyone

**What makes a good contribution:**
- Grounded in the reality of service organizations — not tech company assumptions
- Practical over theoretical — if it doesn't help someone on a Monday morning, it's probably not right for this framework
- Plain language — the framework should be readable by a non-technical leader
